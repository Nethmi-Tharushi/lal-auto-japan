How to Maintain Your Vehicle for Long-Term Value

Auto Traders Team  |  02.09.2025  |  5 comments


1. Regular service schedule

Follow your manufacturer’s maintenance intervals — typically every 5,000 to 10,000 km. Regular oil and filter changes prevent engine wear and improve efficiency.

2. Keep it clean

Washing your car regularly protects paint and prevents rust, especially in coastal or rainy areas. Clean interiors also retain value when reselling.

3. Monitor tire condition

Check tire pressure monthly and rotate every 10,000 km. Properly inflated tires improve safety and fuel economy.

4. Watch your fluids

Inspect engine oil, coolant, brake, and transmission fluids regularly. Top up or replace as needed to avoid mechanical failures.

5. Drive smart

Avoid harsh acceleration and braking. Smooth driving extends engine life and saves fuel.

6. Keep records

Maintain a file of invoices and service logs. This transparency builds trust and can significantly increase resale value.

Final thought

A well-maintained vehicle isn’t just reliable — it’s worth more when you sell it. At Lal Auto Traders, we value vehicles that show consistent care and offer maintenance support through our partner network.
