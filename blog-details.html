Top Tips for Buying a Used Car in Sri Lanka

Auto Traders Team  |  10.09.2025  |  8 comments


1. Verify the vehicle history

Ask for the service history, previous ownership details, and any records of major repairs or insurance claims. A clear service history is one of the best indicators that a car has been well maintained.

2. Do a thorough inspection

Check the body for signs of repair (uneven gaps, repainting), look for rust in the sills and undercarriage, and inspect tires for even wear. Open the bonnet ‚Äî look for oil leaks, corrosion, and belt condition. If you‚Äôre not confident, bring a trusted mechanic to inspect the car.

3. Test drive with purpose

During the test drive, listen for unusual noises from the engine or transmission, test the brakes at different speeds, and try the car on a mix of city and open-road conditions. Pay attention to steering feel and gearbox shifts.

4. Verify paperwork

Confirm ownership using the registration document, and verify engine and chassis numbers match. Check for outstanding loans or liens on the vehicle, and make sure the NOC (if required) is available for transfers.

5. Check market value & negotiate

6. Consider running costs
